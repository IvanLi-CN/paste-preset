# Product

## Register

product

## Users
PastePreset serves people who regularly clean up screenshots, mobile photos, and shared images before sending them into chat tools, docs, tickets, or design workflows. They are usually working quickly inside a browser tab, often while switching between communication, documentation, and asset-prep tasks, and they want the tool to feel immediate instead of ceremonial.

## Product Purpose
PastePreset exists to turn image cleanup into a fast browser-native step: paste, adjust a preset, and export a share-ready result without uploading anything to a server. Success means the interface feels trustworthy, local-first, and efficient enough that users reach for it as a routine utility instead of opening a heavier editor.

## Brand Personality
PastePreset should feel bright, capable, and friendly. Its tone is practical and confident, with just enough energy to make image preparation feel lightweight rather than technical or intimidating.

## Anti-references
PastePreset should not look like a generic dark SaaS admin, a neon hacker console, or a warm beige AI landing page pretending to be a productivity tool. It should also avoid over-decorated creative-suite chrome that makes a quick clipboard utility feel slower or more complex than it is.

## Design Principles
- Keep the workflow legible at a glance so paste-to-export feels immediate.
- Let the brand add energy without obscuring trusted product affordances.
- Treat privacy and local-first behavior as part of the product's visual credibility.
- Use bright, controlled emphasis to guide action instead of flooding the whole surface with accent color.
- Make responsive states feel intentionally designed, not merely collapsed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
PastePreset targets WCAG AA contrast for body text, controls, and feedback states across both light and dark themes. Motion should remain subtle and respect reduced-motion preferences, and status/error surfaces should stay readable for users who rely on keyboard navigation, strong focus cues, and clear semantic color differences.
